Which 3D aesthetic feels right for your portfolio?

The 3D elements will be subtle and enhance the premium feel without being distracting

A

Abstract Geometric Mesh

Vibe: Sophisticated, architectural, mysterious

Floating low-poly geometric forms that slowly morph and rotate. Soft lighting creates depth. Very Linear.app / Apple-esque.

Pros

  • Extremely premium look
  • Lightweight performance
  • Timeless aesthetic

Cons

  • Less interactive
  • May feel cold if overdone
B

Interactive Particle Field

Vibe: Dynamic, alive, responsive

Thousands of small particles that react to mouse movement and scroll. Creates a sense of depth and interactivity. Think Stripe meets Awwwards.

Pros

  • Highly engaging
  • Shows technical skill
  • Fun to interact with

Cons

  • Can be performance-heavy
  • Risk of feeling gimmicky
C

Depth Layers with Subtle Motion

Vibe: Cinematic, editorial, refined

Multiple layered planes with parallax depth. Ambient camera movement. Soft gradients and lighting. Very Vercel / Ray.so style.

Pros

  • Best performance
  • Most versatile
  • Easy to maintain premium feel

Cons

  • Less "wow" factor
  • Requires excellent execution to stand out
D

Hybrid Approach

Vibe: Best of all worlds, tailored per section

Hero has geometric mesh, scroll sections use depth layers, project showcases have interactive particles. Each section gets what fits best.

Pros

  • Maximum impact
  • Keeps experience fresh
  • Shows range of skills

Cons

  • Most complex to implement
  • Larger bundle size
  • Needs careful cohesion

RECOMMENDATION

Option C (Depth Layers) for a 13-year-old showcasing real production work. It's sophisticated, performs well, and lets your actual projects be the hero. The restraint shows maturity and lets recruiters focus on your work quality.

That said, Option D (Hybrid) could be incredible if we nail the execution - it would really show your technical range.
